Overtaking Competitors

Good UX helps ranking

When Google likes you, customers find you.

It's very competitive online. Ranking on Google takes time, so good user experience matters. Nobody can promise you pole position overnight but we can certainly improve your odds of being seen by the right audience.

Two simple ways to improve your ranking :

1. The most direct way is being faster. Between two similar sites with similar content and domain authority, Google favours the one with better PageSpeed scores and Core Web Vitals.

2. When users search with keywords and find you, you want to keep them on your website. If it's well-structured, well-written and responsive, they engage more and navigate to internal links (Longer sessions also make them more likely to contact you).

Higher engagement and click-through rates signal to Google that your content is valuable — this improves your ranking and creates a positive feedback loop.

Bulletproof and Future-proof

Smarter design in the age of AI

WordPress, while popular, presents challenges for AI. Its complex database structure, plugin dependencies, and bloated code make it harder for AI systems to parse content efficiently. Security vulnerabilities, the need for constant updates, and performance issues prevent websites from being prioritized in AI-powered search.

Security First

Did you know?

→ WordPress sites get hacked every 39 seconds on average.
→ 73% of WordPress installations are vulnerable to attacks.
→ Average WordPress site has 22 plugins installed.
→ Plugin conflicts cause 80% of WordPress crashes.

  • Security by Design

    WordPress sites create multiple attack vectors through databases, plugins, and server-side processing. Hackers target WordPress because it's everywhere, and vulnerabilities are well-documented. Miss an update, and you're exposed.

    Static sites eliminate these risks entirely because their architecture itself is the security. Without databases, server-side code or plugins, there's simply nothing for hackers to exploit. Just clean HTML, CSS and JavaScript files delivered via superfast CDN (Content Delivery Network).
