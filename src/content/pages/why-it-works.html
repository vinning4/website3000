// The Architecture

Coded manually. No templates.

Hand-coded means exactly that — every line written deliberately, nothing generated by a template engine or assembled from third-party plugins. The result is pure HTML and CSS files served directly to the browser: no database queries, no server-side processing, nothing running in the background that can slow down, break, or be hacked.

For a business owner, this translates simply: a faster site, more secure, and one that's superior to the competition.

// The Details

Frequently asked questions.

How does website subscription work?

Like a mobile or internet plan. Unlike traditional web design — which typically means a large upfront fee with no support for future edits or maintenance — the subscription is an ongoing service to get a professional website designed, built and maintained by a developer. Includes everything: hosting, updates, edits, SEO improvements, security, email forwarding for contact form submissions. One monthly fee, one direct line to the developer, zero technical overhead.

What is the minimum subscription length?

12 months, then month-to-month with no lock-in. The initial term gives the site time to establish performance and search visibility.

Do I need to manage anything technical?

Nothing. Everything is explained in plain English. I handle the technical side so you can focus on your business. Reach out anytime if you have questions.

Why don't you use WordPress, Squarespace, or Wix?

Those platforms are built for everyone, so they're optimised for no one. They rely on themes, plugins, and databases — introducing bloat, performance drag, and security vulnerabilities. At Website3000, every site is pure hand-written HTML and CSS: no databases to hack, no surprise breakdowns, no sudden mandatory upgrades, and no unused code slowing down the site. For businesses, this means a website that loads instantly, works reliably on all devices, and has predictable costs over time — without the overhead that often comes with WordPress setups.

What's this about Google PSI scores?

PSI = PageSpeed Insights. It's a Google tool that evaluates website performance on mobile and desktop. Page speed affects user experience and is a confirmed search ranking signal, since slow sites increase abandonment. Scores above 90 are considered strong; 95–100 indicates a superior level of optimisation.

What happens if I cancel the subscription after 12 months?

When the subscription ends, hosting and maintenance stop, and the website is taken offline — because the service that keeps it running is no longer active. Your domain is always yours to keep. If you'd prefer to continue using the website without a subscription, you can purchase it outright via a buyout option. This gives you full ownership of the code files, and you're free to manage or host the site elsewhere.

How does the subscription compare to paying upfront?

The upfront option ($3,000) delivers the site and full source code at handover so you can manage it independently. Outsourcing small changes or maintenance usually involves hourly developer fees. The subscription spreads the cost and includes an on-call developer actively managing the site. Over time the total spend is similar — the difference is that everything is handled for you.

How can the website pay for itself?

If your website generates just one new customer per month worth $150 or more, the subscription effectively covers itself. For many service businesses such as trades and professional services, a single new customer is often worth several hundred dollars, which means the website doesn't need high traffic to generate positive returns. If your current website isn't generating these results, an upgrade helps.

How does SEO work?

Both pricing options include on-page Search Engine Optimisation. This means your site is structured so search engines like Google can read it clearly — through logical page hierarchy, optimised titles and descriptions, fast load times, mobile-friendly layouts, lightweight image formats, and clean semantic code. Local business information is included so Google understands what your business does and where it operates.
