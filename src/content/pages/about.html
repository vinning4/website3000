  • About Your Site: What Matters

    An effective website functions as a commercial instrument. It helps the right audience find you, understand your offer quickly, and act. For most businesses, this requires just 3 to 5 fast, well-structured pages. Yet the prevailing options fall short: bloated, template-driven WordPress builds, usually low cost, or agencies encumbered by process, overhead, and jargon—usually expensive. Both approaches miss the point. Your website isn't a vanity project, it's digital real estate that reflects standards.

    A third path is DIY. Creating a site is easy, but effectiveness asks more: does it attract qualified visitors, load quickly across devices, and send clear signals to Google? When built with technical discipline, a website becomes a durable asset.

  • How I Work

    I focus on what matters: fast-loading websites designed to capture enquiries and render accurately on any device. I write every line of code in semantic HTML, CSS and JavaScript without templates or frameworks — an increasingly uncommon approach that allows direct control over the look and functionality. The result is a simpler, more dependable system: no unnecessary layers, no databases to expose, no plugins that introduce failure points.
