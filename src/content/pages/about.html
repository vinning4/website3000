// About Me

I'm Vin, the software engineer behind Website3000. With a background in bioengineering, molecular biology, and product design, I approach web development with a function-first mindset — building sites designed to turn visitors into customers. Formally trained in front-end development, I create websites because I genuinely enjoy coding.

Too many small business websites still fail at the basics — slow, dated and neglected, or over-designed and unclear. A good website is not optional; it's where trust is won or lost. Based in Melbourne AU, I run an independent practice helping service businesses — without agency markup.

// About Websites

The true measure of a business website is performance, not complexity. Yet most sites are built once and forgotten. Slow on mobile. Invisible to Google. Designed to show existence rather than serve a visitor. Quietly, it becomes a liability dressed as an asset.

An effective website functions as a commercial instrument. It helps the right audience find you, understand your offer quickly, and act. For most businesses, this requires just 3 to 5 fast, well-structured pages. Yet the prevailing options fall short: bloated, low-cost, template-driven WordPress builds, or high-dollar agencies encumbered by process, overhead, and jargon. Both approaches miss the point. Your website isn't a vanity project, it's digital real estate that reflects standards.

A third path is DIY. While publishing a site is easy, true effectiveness requires technical discipline. Done right, a website becomes a durable asset.

// The Work

Website3000 is different. Every site is hand-coded — no templates, no plugins, no unnecessary complexity. I focus on what matters: fast-loading websites designed to capture enquiries and render accurately on any device. I write every line of code in semantic HTML, CSS and JavaScript — an increasingly uncommon approach that allows direct control over the look and functionality. The result is a simpler, more dependable system: no bloated layers, no databases to hack, no plugins that crash.

The subscription model exists because a website is not finished at launch. It is a living operation that should evolve with a business. Edits, SEO, performance monitoring, and ongoing management are included — not as extras, but as the point.

// The Practice

Clients get a site that loads in one second, scores 95–100 on Google PageSpeed, and is designed to turn visitors into enquiries. You also work directly with me as I manage your site — no call queues, no support tickets, no handoffs.

This is independent practice at its most deliberate — each client served well, and completely. Not scaled for volume. Calibrated for quality.
